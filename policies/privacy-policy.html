Privacy Policy for Soundboard Studio

Last updated: December 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how the Soundboard Studio application uses permissions and processes data while you use the app.

2. Use of Microphone Permission (RECORD_AUDIO)

Our application requires access to the microphone solely for the purpose of recording custom audio clips directly within the app. This permission allows users to create their own soundboard buttons with recorded sounds.

Important: The microphone is used only when the user explicitly starts a recording. The application never records audio in the background without your knowledge.

3. How Recordings Are Processed

All audio recordings created within the app are:

  • Stored locally – all recordings are saved exclusively on your device
  • Under your control – you can delete them at any time within the app
  • Private – recordings never leave your device

4. What We Do NOT Do

To protect your privacy, we explicitly state that the application:

  • DOES NOT STORE recordings on external servers or in the cloud
  • DOES NOT COLLECT any personal data
  • DOES NOT SHARE recordings with third parties
  • DOES NOT SEND any analytics or tracking data
  • DOES NOT REQUIRE registration or account creation
  • DOES NOT INCLUDE third-party tracking tools

5. Data Storage

All data (including recordings, button settings, and preferences) is stored locally on your device. No data is transmitted over the internet.

6. Your Rights

You have full control over your data:

  • You can revoke microphone permission at any time in your device settings
  • You can delete any recordings directly within the app
  • You can uninstall the app, which will remove all locally stored data

7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

If we make changes to how the application processes data, we will update this Privacy Policy and the “last updated” date. We recommend reviewing this page periodically for any changes.

Advertising

The application displays advertisements provided by third-party advertising services (such as Google AdMob).

These services may use device identifiers (including the Advertising ID) to show ads that are relevant to the user.

The application itself does not collect or store any personal data related to advertising. Any data used for advertising purposes is handled in accordance with the privacy policies of the advertising providers.
